## Key Topics:
- Bitcoin's global addressable market: ~$1,000 trillion in total world asset value across real estate, bonds, equities, money, gold, and art
- Bitcoin currently represents only 0.2% of global asset value, framing the scale of the opportunity
- Jesse's original model projects Bitcoin reaching 20% of global assets; Saylor's more aggressive model targets 50%
- Saylor's $13M per Bitcoin (20-year projection) assumes 4x nominal asset inflation and Bitcoin at 7% of global assets, with further upside to 50%
- Bitcoin treasury companies as securitised Bitcoin exposure, offering equity investors ~29% annual returns vs. ~6% on the LSE
- MicroStrategy's preferred equity instruments (11.5% yield) competing directly with the $370 trillion fixed income market
- The "multiplier effect": $1 of Bitcoin buying drives roughly $1–4 of price appreciation depending on market cycle timing
- Cold storage Bitcoin as the "risk-free rate" for Bitcoin maximalists, with Bitcoin treasury companies serving as the risk capital layer
- The case against Bitcoin maximalist dogma: AI and high-growth equities can legitimately outperform Bitcoin for skilled stock-pickers
- Bitcoin's outsized returns are time-limited — expected to diminish as it matures into a store-of-value asset class over the next 15–20+ years

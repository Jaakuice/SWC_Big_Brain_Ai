## Key Topics:
- Bitcoin as a superior store of value versus fiat currencies, which Andrew argues have been "badly managed"
- The origin and mechanics of Bitcoin treasury companies, pioneered by Michael Saylor's MicroStrategy/Strategy in 2020
- Smarter Web Company's IPO journey: from listing at 2.12p on Aquis, surging to over £5 (UK's best-performing IPO in 20+ years), falling to ~60p
- mNAV (multiple of Net Asset Value) as the key valuation metric for Bitcoin treasury companies
- FCA regulation, Bitcoin ETNs, and the barriers retail investors face buying Bitcoin in the UK
- Distinguishing Bitcoin from other cryptocurrencies, and the "4-year hold" argument against losses
- Skepticism about the multiplier effect between a Bitcoin treasury and an operating business
- The "crypto bro" culture perception versus the reality of Bitcoin conference attendees
- Personal security concerns for executives holding large corporate Bitcoin reserves
- Andrew's ambition to list on the London Stock Exchange and eventually become one of the UK's largest companies

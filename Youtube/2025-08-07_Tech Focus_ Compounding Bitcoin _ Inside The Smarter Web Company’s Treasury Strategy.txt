## Key Topics:
- SWC's Bitcoin holdings growth from ~30-50 BTC to 2,050 BTC in two months, with cash reserves for 2,300+ BTC
- Three capital-raising strategies: private placements, ATM (at-the-money) facility, and the new "Smarter Convert" instrument
- Smarter Convert explained: a 0% interest convertible bond with a 5% premium conversion price, 12-month duration, Bitcoin-denominated, backed by a named institutional investor
- Andrew's meeting with Michael Saylor (Strategy) and lessons from larger Bitcoin treasury companies
- mNAV analysis: SWC trading back to May 1st levels, considered compelling value vs. MetaPlanet, Strategy, and Capital B
- Bitcoin downside risk: shares would suffer but no debt exposure; worst case is returning Bitcoin rather than fiat
- Retail access to the convertible bond: currently restricted to high-net-worth/professional investors; listed retail products (like Strategy's preferred shares) are a longer-term ambition
- Additional liquidity pools: US listing, ATM, Frankfurt stock line, and future unannounced initiatives
- The "compound Bitcoin" thesis: holding SWC stock as a way to gain leveraged/compounding BTC exposure vs. holding BTC directly
- SWC's zero-debt position and 15-year operating history as a foundation for the treasury strategy

## Key Topics:
- SWC (The Smarter Web Company) offers UK investors a new tax-efficient (ISA/SIPP) route to Bitcoin exposure via a listed equity, since Bitcoin ETFs/ETNs aren't yet available to UK retail investors
- Since its April 25, 2025 IPO at 2.5p, SWC shares have surged over 100x to £340+, making it the best-performing stock in the UK and largest by market cap on the Aquis exchange
- SWC's Bitcoin treasury strategy mirrors MicroStrategy (Strategy)'s model pioneered by Michael Saylor, but without using leverage
- Key drivers of success: rapid Bitcoin accumulation pace (low "days to cover" MNAV of 43 days), a retail-focused funding model via Winterflood "wraps," first-mover advantage in the UK market, a new US OTC listing, and founder Andrew Webley's strong Bitcoin conviction
- Potential future catalysts include a possible London Stock Exchange listing and FTSE 250/FTSE 100 index inclusion, which could unlock major passive capital inflows
- Key risks: slowing accumulation pace as the company grows, emerging competitors (e.g., Coinsium Group's "Forza"), high volatility and potential sharp drawdowns, and an expensive valuation (MNAV of 9-14x vs. Strategy's ~1.7x)
- The presenter views SWC as a momentum trade rather than a pure Bitcoin proxy, and personally prefers MSTR/Strategy for more correlated Bitcoin exposure

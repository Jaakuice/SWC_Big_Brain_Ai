## Key Topics:

- Michael Saylor/MicroStrategy as the defining moment that clarified Bitcoin's potential
- Raising £200M in ~3.5 months via the UK's first Bitcoin treasury IPO
- Skepticism from UK capital markets and why they initially "didn't get it"
- Accepting Bitcoin as payment in 2023 as an early bold step
- Smarter Convert: the UK's first Bitcoin-denominated bond instrument

**RoxomTV Host:**
(0:00:00) So first up I would love to hear a little bit about your career trajectory and sort of how and when you discovered Bitcoin and how they have kind of moved together over the years.

**Andrew Webley:**
(0:00:12) Yeah, sure. So I started my career in 1999. I joined a very small company which became one of the biggest companies in the UK, Hargreaves Lansdown. So I did that for 10 years. And then I decided to leave HL and being a developer, I wanted to build a system which I could then build a business around. So I built a content management system which is the same system that we use today to design and build and host websites for our clients. We think it's a very good system, very easy to use, very affordable. And then it got to about almost 10 years ago and I found Bitcoin. And being honest, when I first found it, the significance of Bitcoin didn't quite click. I thought it was a volatile asset, a good medium of exchange, these sorts of things.

**RoxomTV Host:**
(0:00:56) How did you find it? Did somebody tell you about it?

**Andrew Webley:**
(0:00:58) Yeah, I can't remember the finer points of how I found it. The only thing I can really remember is that it was really hard to buy at the time. It was before the big exchanges. So what I used to do is I used to have to buy it and then send the money through the bank — which was actually easier than it is some of the time today — and then you'd get it into an online wallet. Completely crazy compared with what it is now. I think it was 2017, something like that.
(0:01:31) But for me the defining moment was going back to my time at HL — I was very clued up on the public markets and I'd been investing, sometimes well, sometimes not so well. And the moment for me was when Michael Saylor came out with MicroStrategy's Bitcoin adoption. They basically took a public company and Bitcoin and smashed the two together. And for me that was like a lightning moment. It was just the best thing ever. And that's when the significance really really clicked.
(0:02:02) So going back to the Smarter Web Company — we've always been profitable, we've always enjoyed it, we've got lots and lots of good clients, over 250 clients. It's a good business, just a small business. But what I decided was that I wanted to invest in a UK version of Strategy. And there wasn't one, not one that I could find. This is about two and a half, three years ago. And I kept looking every single day trying to find a company that I could invest some of my money in — I still hold Strategy today, in fact I bought some more this morning — but I just wanted a UK version of it. There are lots of reasons why that's the right thing in my opinion for someone to want. And I couldn't find it. Then fast forwarding to last year, shortly after the summer, I still couldn't find it. So I decided, probably end of Q3, start of Q4, that I would list my own business.
(0:03:02) And here we are today. We started work on that sort of December, properly in January. We listed in April this year. I must admit at the time everyone thought that I was mad — all the people in the city, they didn't think there was anything that could be done, even though I was saying to them, look at Strategy, look at MetaPlanet. One of our directors is on the board of MetaPlanet. It's there for everyone to see.

**RoxomTV Host:**
(0:03:29) Do you think they thought it was such a crazy idea just because of the regulatory landscape in the UK?

**Andrew Webley:**
(0:03:35) I'm not sure whether it was so much on the regulatory side. I think the UK capital markets have had such a torrid time for however long it is — 10, 15 years. We've raised — give or take around about 200 million pounds, probably a little bit more than that to be fair, or actually maybe slightly less — call it 200 million pounds. We raised 200 million pounds in three and a half months. And I just don't think they could see the potential. Obviously I could see it. We're still tiny compared with where I want us to be. But they just didn't really get it, on the whole.
(0:04:33) And then what we did is we wanted to approach it differently to most public companies. It's a business that I founded, I care about it. I often say that I've got two kids and this is like the one in the middle — so three kids. And so we wanted to do it with hard work, with transparency, with integrity. Everyone's seen how hard we've worked as a team so far to try and get to where we are today. And I think that's one of the reasons why people have supported it — because they like the fact that we care about it. It means something to us. And we've done it.

(0:06:21) So when we decided to list the business, I didn't have any doubt in my mind. Zero doubt. And funnily enough my wife, who was a 50% shareholder of the Smarter Web Company with me and would obviously convert those shares into Smarter Web Company shares when we did the transaction — she had no doubt either. The reality of what you've got with a Bitcoin treasury company is you need increasingly larger and larger amounts of capital. So the question is how are you going to do that?
(0:10:20) Take MetaPlanet as a good example — which I also bought some more shares of on Friday. The way you do it is you demonstrate to the people that provide the capital that you are delivering a compound Bitcoin strategy — i.e. producing a yield, Bitcoin per share increase, whatever you want to call it. (0:11:10) So that capital can be done in lots of different ways. People can buy our equity within a raise, within the market. If they buy it within the market they might be buying from somebody selling the shares, or they might be buying effectively from our ATM on an update. We've also got something that we announced about two weeks ago called Smarter Convert. That's the first, as far as I'm aware, Bitcoin-denominated bond the UK has ever seen. We've got other things which I'm not allowed to talk about, which we're exploring for the future — in addition to the medium to long-term goal of having preference shares when we get, let's say, 10 times bigger or maybe a little bit more. That's something that's very, very important, but it's not a today thing. There's a cost to providing those products; the market needs to be a bit more mature until you can do that.
(0:12:01) I think the other angle that people need to look at — being completely transparent — is that as you get bigger, those capital-raising announcements that you make may get more frequent, sometimes smaller, and sometimes bigger. (0:12:35) If you look at our company, we're still at 76% or whatever the number is over the last 30-day period — we're still almost doubling the Bitcoin per share over that 30-day period. The trick with it all is looking at that profile and our investors saying: do they believe that we can continue the progress that we've got? And that's where the work comes in. We work very very long days trying to get everything done. What I'm doing at the moment compared with a month ago — it's busier. The Bitcoin per share is greater. The share price is lower.

- Bitcoin yield generation strategies: routing fees, lending, and leveraged balance sheets

That for me is fundamentally important. So thinking about Bitcoin through a payment lens — we all know that the base layer, the UTXO set, isn't capable of giving censorship-resistant money to everyone on Earth. Not all of us can hold our own keys. And quite frankly, the settlement time — the gap between your transaction speed and your settlement speed on the base layer — is probably always going to be too long for you to buy a cup of coffee. That was always one of the main criticisms in the early days of Bitcoin being used as money. Now we know there are a lot of different second-layer solutions to enable instantaneous transactions and instantaneous settlements. The Lightning Network is the biggest and the most well-developed of those. For anyone who's not familiar with it, there are two base layer transactions in order to create a lightning channel — one when you open the channel and one when you close it. And for the period that the channel is open, the two participants — let's say Steph and I have a channel here — we can make as many different payments between each other in real time as we like. But the cool thing about the network is it's not just one payment channel. If there's someone at the back of the room to whom I want to send money but I'm not connected to them, but Steph is, then I can route through Steph. And much in the same way as you may be familiar with Visa and Mastercard interchange fees — which are relatively high by comparison — Steph would then be able to charge a small routing fee for the privilege of enabling me to pay that person at the back. And that's essentially how our first business model works. We are setting up a large number of well-capitalised, well-funded routing nodes at specific junctures on the Lightning Network that enable us to optimise for the routing of payments. At the moment we're seeing about 6% return on invested capital and crucially — because we self-custody and have proof of reserves on our website — there is limited to no counterparty risk. So back to the credit idea: no one's being trusted here. That Bitcoin locked in the channel is still ours and if anything goes wrong with the channel, it will come back to our self-custody address within about six blocks and at the absolute maximum about two weeks.

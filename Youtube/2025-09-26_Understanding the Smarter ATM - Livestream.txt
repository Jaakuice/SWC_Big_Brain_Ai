## Key Topics:
- MNAV to BTC yield relationship: higher MNAV produces exponentially higher BTC yield per share
- ATM simulation methodology: three acquisition methods (ATM, cash-secured puts, preferred shares), MNAV-driven stock price modeling
- Real-world ATM price impact data from MetaPlanet: ~1% temporary MNAV reduction when ATM is operating
- Comparison of 20% vs 10% vs 5% ATM sizes: larger ATM significantly outperforms over time despite dilution
- "Seizing the MNAV moment": why companies must actively run ATMs during high-MNAV periods rather than sitting on their hands
- Floor price model: aggressive ATM use compounds Bitcoin holdings and raises the floor price at future MNAV troughs
- Preferred share (prep) IPO modelling: $100M base case vs $1B bull case, timed around 10,000 BTC milestone
- LSE uplisting as a potential catalyst for large institutional equity raises and accelerated BTC accumulation
- Simulation limitations: models break down beyond 2030 as projected BTC accumulation rates become physically impossible
- Bear vs bull MNAV scenarios: base case assumes pain (drops to ~1 MNAV), bull case peaks at 5–7 with 900M+ shares outstanding

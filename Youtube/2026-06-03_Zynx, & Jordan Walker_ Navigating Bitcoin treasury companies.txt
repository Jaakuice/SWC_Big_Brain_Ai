## Key Topics:
- Bitcoin self-custody as the primary goal, with treasury companies being supplementary
- Bitcoin treasury companies as financial engineering operations performing a carry trade against fiat debasement
- The Japanese yen carry trade as an analogy for Bitcoin treasury strategy
- Preferred shares and perpetual debt instruments (e.g. STRC, Strike) as tools for Bitcoin accumulation
- Criteria for evaluating Bitcoin treasury companies: profitability and fixed income product offerings
- Future of the space: increased competition, innovation, and products eating into the fixed income market
- UK-specific developments: preference share issuance by domestic companies
- Generating yield on Bitcoin as a key challenge for treasury companies
- Falcon Edge highlighted as an interesting yield-generating model
- The race among financial engineers to maximise the fiat-to-Bitcoin arbitrage

**Zynx:**
(0:02:01) So Bitcoin treasury is essentially a game of whoever are the best financial engineers who can perform the best carry trade. The total addressable market cap for Bitcoin right now is 300 trillion dollars — it's 1 trillion dollars right now, so there's a massive arbitrage. I'm essentially selling the weaker currency, fiat, and accumulating as much Bitcoin as possible, the hardest asset. And to sum it up quite succinctly, whoever are the best financial engineers stand to benefit the most from that arbitrage. A perfect real-life example is the Japanese yen carry trade — institutions for decades have been borrowing in the debasing currency at super low interest rates and making a profit by investing that currency in assets that are higher yielding. And essentially it's the same thing — the primary goal of a Bitcoin treasury company is to benefit from that arbitrage between fiat, which is constantly debasing, and Bitcoin.

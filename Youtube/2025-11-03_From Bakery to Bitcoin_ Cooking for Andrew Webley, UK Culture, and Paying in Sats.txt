## Key Topics:
- Henry Herbert's fifth-generation family bakery background and transition to running Bitcoin-accepting supper clubs in Bristol
- Hosting Andrew Webley (CEO, Smarter Web Company) at sold-out candlelit dinners attended by shareholders traveling from across the UK
- UK Bitcoin culture compared to the US — communal dinner-table gatherings vs. corporate conferences, with shareholder fandom mirroring soccer club loyalty
- Why Smarter Web became a rallying point for UK Bitcoiners starved of a domestic Strategy-equivalent
- Accepting Bitcoin in practice: Bridge to Bitcoin onboarding, Wallet of Satoshi, and the Musket all-in-one POS terminal
- Accounting and tax considerations for small businesses earning in Bitcoin
- The psychological shift from "silent stacker" to active Bitcoin earner/spender and how it transformed Henry's network
- Henry's ambition to cook on Michael Saylor's yacht — and the guest at dinner who works for Nakamoto and was on the yacht a month prior
- Plans for a Bitcoin Feast on Tour across US Bitcoin hubs (Nashville, Austin, Miami, etc.)
- Encouragement for UK small businesses: the infrastructure now exists and small operators are most agile to adopt

**Jesse Myers (Bitcoin Strategy, Smarter Web Company):**
(0:14:26) All right. Great. Thank you, John and Nick. So we are a web design company. We are also a Bitcoin treasury company. As you all know, we believe that Bitcoin is the best asset in the world. (0:15:12) So I'll talk a little bit about Bitcoin and Bitcoin treasury companies and Smarter Web as a Bitcoin treasury company. There are different ways you can get exposure to Bitcoin — you can hold Bitcoin, you can hold Bitcoin in an ETF, you can buy Bitcoin related companies, or you can own shares in a Bitcoin treasury company. We believe that the Bitcoin treasury company model offers the most attractive way to get exposure to Bitcoin because the only thing better than Bitcoin is more Bitcoin. And our view is that the mechanics and tools available to a Bitcoin treasury company to increase Bitcoin per share make it more attractive than any of the other ways that you can get exposure to Bitcoin. However, this comes with volatility — which feels great on the way up, feels less good in downtrends as we're all aware recently. But volatility is vitality for Bitcoin treasury companies and Bitcoin in general.

(0:16:13) So this is my personal view of Bitcoin's volatility and how it moves as a market. Bitcoin is cyclical. It goes through cycles of sentiment. It feels great at times, it feels terrible at other times. And Bitcoin treasury companies, as we've seen, are downstream of Bitcoin sentiment. And my view is that in times when people want more Bitcoin, the premium — the mNAV levels for Bitcoin treasury companies — can go up, and in times when people want less Bitcoin exposure, those premiums can evaporate. And that's okay — it's cyclical, and there are opportunities at different stages of those cycles to execute on our strategy to increase Bitcoin per share at any level. Of course, it's a little easier when mNAVs are higher.

(0:17:07) So the net of that is that at different times there will be different levels of mNAV — of premium or discount to our net asset value, meaning our Bitcoin treasury. This is our view of how investors value a Bitcoin treasury company. Of course there is the hard and fast value of your Bitcoin on your balance sheet, but subjectively at all times, investors are thinking about and evaluating the expected future growth of Bitcoin per share and weighing that against the expected drag of admin of a public company — of an entity rather than just holding Bitcoin themselves.

(0:17:57) On average, our view is that there will be a recognition that there's the capacity to increase Bitcoin per share over time. And on average, there should be, in our view, an mNAV premium that we can then utilize to make the most of the strategy and harvest to increase Bitcoin per share. But at times the subjective valuations of those two key buckets will be different. When people are bearish about Bitcoin or sentiment has evaporated, they are similarly unenthusiastic at times about the capacity to increase Bitcoin per share forever into the future for Bitcoin treasury companies — and it might look like something on the left, which is closest to where our current mNAV is now. But at other times it'll be neutral or even bullish, and the expectation of future growth in Bitcoin per share may be quite high and the willingness to pay for participating in that may be quite high. All this to say that it is cyclical and there are times when it will feel great and times when it won't feel good — and that's all part of the volatility that a Bitcoin treasury company delivers, which is the essential element for performance as an asset class and for portfolios.

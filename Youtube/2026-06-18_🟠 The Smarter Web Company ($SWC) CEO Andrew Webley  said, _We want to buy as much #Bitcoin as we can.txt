## Key Topics:
- SWC has acquired approximately 3,000 Bitcoin since going public ~13 months ago
- Raised nearly £250 million and deployed it into Bitcoin regardless of price, resulting in a debt-free balance sheet
- Recently used a Coinbase credit facility to add ~15% leverage to buy more Bitcoin
- Executed a warrant buyback equivalent to purchasing Bitcoin at ~$49,000 — below market price at the time
- Andrew questions why more mid-sized Bitcoin treasury companies haven't added responsible leverage at this point in the cycle
- DCA strategy is optimal for individuals but not necessarily for companies with clean balance sheets
- SWC's thesis: Bitcoin will rise significantly, making current fiat liabilities relatively cheaper over time
- Emphasis on "responsible" leverage — amplification without undue balance sheet stress

**Andrew Webley (CEO, Smarter Web Company):**
(0:00:00) Um, the thing though that really stands out to me — not completely moving on from what you said, Tim — is that I don't understand why more of the sort of mid-sized Bitcoin treasury companies like ourselves haven't added responsible leverage at this moment in the cycle. Because what we did — so we became a public company about 13 months ago — and we've bought up to about 3,000 Bitcoin, just below, as of today. And what we did is we raised quite a lot of money, almost a quarter of a billion pounds, and we deployed that into Bitcoin regardless of the price. And what that gave us was a balance sheet with no debt.

(0:00:53) So what we did earlier this year — I think it was maybe six to eight weeks ago — is we did some equity style transactions where we bought some warrants back at a discount, which was like a share buyback, which was the equivalent of buying Bitcoin for about $49,000 at the time. A big discount to where it was trading at that point in time, and still a transaction that's in the money now. We bought the bottom when the market didn't even go that low, if you want to put it like that. But then we continued to use that Coinbase credit facility to put some responsible leverage onto the balance sheet to buy more Bitcoin, because we're of the opinion that whether it's today, tomorrow, or 3 months' time, Bitcoin is going to go up a lot and those fiat liabilities are going to remain flat.

(0:01:41) So I understand why the DCA strategy can't be implemented by a company as you may think — Jesse's 100% right that it's the best way to buy for an individual, that's why we always use it in our examples — but for a company that believes in Bitcoin and has managed their balance sheet efficiently, they should be adding that leverage right now. As far as I'm concerned, that's what we've done. We've got approximately 15% leverage or amplification on the balance sheet from that facility, structured on the basis that we believe Bitcoin is going to go up considerably and we want to buy as much of it at this level as we can without putting any undue stress on the balance sheet.
